Historical Western Europe

Paris, France - From Medieval Power to Modern Capital

Founded in the 3rd century BC by Celtic settlers, Paris became a center of power under the Capetian dynasty. From medieval Notre-Dame to the French Revolution, Paris has shaped European culture, politics, and art for centuries.

Historical Western Europe

Amsterdam, Netherlands - Golden Age of Trade

Established as a fishing village in the 12th century, Amsterdam rose to prominence during the Dutch Golden Age of the 17th century, becoming a hub of global trade, finance, and art, home to painters like Rembrandt and Vermeer.

Vienna, Austria - Imperial Capital of the Habsburgs

Vienna flourished as the seat of the powerful Habsburg dynasty, shaping European politics for centuries. From Baroque palaces to Mozart’s symphonies, the city’s history reflects its role as a cultural and imperial powerhouse.

Berlin, Germany - From Prussian Glory to Modern Unity

Once the capital of Prussia and later the German Empire, Berlin witnessed world wars, division during the Cold War, and reunification in 1990. Its history mirrors Europe’s turbulent yet resilient journey.

Munich, Germany - Bavarian Tradition and Royal Heritage

Founded in 1158, Munich became a cultural center under the Wittelsbach dynasty. Famous for Oktoberfest and baroque palaces, the city reflects Bavaria’s royal heritage and resilience after World War II reconstruction.
